abstract: |
  We show that a class of collinear compensated magnets — distinct from the
  momentum-dependent class — produces a non-relativistic spin splitting *at
  the Brillouin-zone centre*. The splitting is due to the breaking of
  combinational crystallographic and magnetic site symmetries, distinguishing
  it from the momentum-dependent splittings that vanish at high-symmetry
  points. We provide material candidates and discuss experimental signatures.
