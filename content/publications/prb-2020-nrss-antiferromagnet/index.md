abstract: |
  We predict that a class of collinear antiferromagnets with spin sublattices not 
  related by inversion nor translation exhibits a *giant, momentum-dependent spin splitting* of the
  electronic bands even in the absence of spin–orbit coupling and without breaking
  inversion symmetry. The splitting is set by the magnetic symmetry of the spin
  arrangement rather than by relativistic effects, can reach 1 eV in low-Z
  compounds, and is purely symmetric under inversion. This work establishes the
  symmetry conditions and provides the early material predictions for what is
  now widely known as *altermagnets*.
